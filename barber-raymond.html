About Raymond

Raymond Ellis grew up sweeping hair off the floor of his father's shop in Oildale, learning the trade the long way: broom first, lather second, clippers when Earl said so. He earned his license, worked years in other people's chairs, and eventually signed the lease on a brick storefront of his own on Panama Lane.

That storefront still looks the part. The brick is original. The bench by the window came out of a diner that closed years back. The barber pole out front is the one Earl handed him the day the shop opened — still turning.

What has changed is the roster. Raymond went from one chair to eight, and he hand-picked every barber in them — some he trained from apprentice, others he recruited after watching them work. He still cuts a full book, still opens the door first thing, and still refuses to rush a shave.

Ask any regular what makes Raymond's different and you'll get the same answer: he remembers. Your part, your cowlick, your kid's name, the fact that you hate clippers around your ears. That's not a system. That's a career of paying attention.

“A haircut is a handshake that lasts three weeks. Do it right.”
